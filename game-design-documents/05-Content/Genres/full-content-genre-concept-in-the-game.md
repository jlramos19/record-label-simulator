`Full content Genre Concept in the Game`

`1. What is a content Genre?`

	`•	A content genre is a connection between a theme and a mood that defines the narrative focus and emotional tone of content.`

	`•	content Genres are represented as:`

	`•	“Theme but make it Mood” (e.g., “ambition but make it uplifting”).`

`2. Slots and Their Role in content Genres`

	`•	Slots: Singular cells in a table, each holding either a theme or a mood.`

	`•	Total Slots per Member/Creator:`

	`•	4 Theme Slots: Dominant traits representing lyrical focus.`

	`•	4 Mood Slots: Recessive traits defining emotional tone.`

	`•	These 8 slots combine to form 4 content genres (one genre per row in the table).`

`3. Behavior of Themes and Moods in Slots`

	`•	Themes (Dominant):`

	`•	Repeated back-to-back in the Theme column.`

	`•	Example: ambition → ambition → loyalty → loyalty.`

	`•	Moods (Recessive):`

	`•	Alternating in the Mood column.`

	`•	Example: uplifting → saddening → uplifting → saddening.`

`4. content Genre Formation in the Table`

`Theme/Mood	Theme	Mood`

`1	ambition	uplifting`

`2	ambition	saddening`

`3	loyalty	uplifting`

`4	loyalty	saddening`

	`•	content Genres (Rows):`

	`1.	ambition but make it uplifting`

	`2.	ambition but make it saddening`

	`3.	loyalty but make it uplifting`

	`4.	loyalty but make it saddening`

`5. How content Genres Relate to Characters`

	`•	Member/Creator Preferences:`

	`•	Members/creators each have 8 slots (4 themes, 4 moods).`

	`•	These preferences define the content genres they create or consume.`

	`•	Preference Development:`

	`1.	Inheritance (First 4 Slots):`

	`•	Members inherit 2 themes and 2 moods from parent members, forming their first 2 content genres.`

	`2.	Consumption Phase (0–3 Age Group):`

	`•	Members gain their second preferred theme through media consumption.`

	`3.	Completion Phase (20–23 Age Group):`

	`•	Members gain their second preferred mood, completing their set of 4 preferred content genres.`

`6. How content Genres Are Used`

	`•	Creators:`

	`•	Use their preferred content genres to guide the themes and moods of the tracks they create.`

	`•	Audience:`

	`•	Audience Chunks prefer specific content genres, influencing what content succeeds in the game.`

	`•	Gameplay Structures:`

	`•	Slots for themes and moods are tied to structures like studios, where creators work to produce content reflecting their preferences.`

`Summary`

	`•	content Genres are rows in a table that combine themes and moods from separate slots.`

	`•	Themes repeat back-to-back, while moods alternate to form distinct content genres.`

	`•	Members/creators develop their preferences through inheritance, media consumption, and life stages, completing a set of 4 preferred content genres by the age of 23.`

	`•	These content genres shape content creation and audience preferences in the game.`
