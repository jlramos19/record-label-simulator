### **`Economy - In-Game Transactions Flow`**

#### **`1. The Government:`**

* **`Role:`** `Builds all necessary structures (institutions, studios, etc.) every 48 years and subsidizes the cost of living for members aged 0-19.`  
* **`Initial Funding:`** `Starts with 1 trillion units.`  
* **`Construction Cost:`** `Constructs institutions and studios, e.g., each studio costs $528,000,000.`  
* **`Revenue Source:`** `Receives rent payments from institutions (tuition from members) and Record Labels (studio leases).`  
* **`Bank Loans:`** `Receives interest-free loans from the bank to subsidize the cost of living for members aged 0-19.`

#### **`2. eyeriS Corporation:`**

* **`Role:`** `Owns and operates institutions (nonprofit) and companies where personnel work (for-profit).`  
* **`Purchasing Structures:`** `Buys structures (like studios) from the government after they are constructed.`  
* **`Loan from Bank:`** `Takes out 30-year loans with fluctuating interest rates to purchase these structures.`  
  * **`Interest Rates:`** `Vary from 0.02% to 50% based on the supply and demand of structures.`  
  * **`High Demand:`** `When structures are scarce and the government builds too few, banks raise interest rates, increasing financial pressure on eyeriS.`  
* **`Revenue Source:`** `Collects rent from institutions and lease payments from Record Labels.`

#### **`3. Record Labels:`**

* **`Role:`** `Lease studios from eyeriS Corporation to produce content.`  
* **`Lease Payments:`** `Pay rent to eyeriS Corporation for the use of studios, which funds eyeriS's loan repayments and operations.`

#### **`4. The Bank:`**

* **`Role:`** `Provides loans to eyeriS Corporation and the government.`  
* **`Loans to eyeriS:`** `Offers 30-year loans with interest rates fluctuating from 0.02% to 50%.`  
* **`Loans to Government:`** `Provides interest-free loans to subsidize the cost of living for members aged 0-19.`

### **`Transaction Flow and Interactions:`**

1. **`Government Builds Structures:`**  
   * `Constructs institutions and studios every 48 years.`  
   * `Example Construction Cost for Studio: $528,000,000.`  
2. **`eyeriS Corporation Buys Structures:`**  
   * `Takes out a 30-year loan from the bank to purchase structures from the government.`  
   * **`Loan Amount:`** `$528,000,000 for each studio.`  
   * **`Interest Rates:`** `Fluctuate between 0.02% to 50% based on market conditions.`  
   * **`Loan Repayment:`** `eyeriS repays the bank with interest over 30 years, using revenue from leases and rent.`  
3. **`Revenue Flow:`**  
   * **`Institutions (Nonprofit):`** `eyeriStudy institutions charge tuition ($1,815 per member per month) paid by the government for members aged 0-19.`  
   * **`Rent to Government:`** `Institutions pay rent to the government, which covers the initial subsidies and infrastructure investment.`  
   * **`Record Labels Lease Studios:`** `Record Labels pay eyeriS Corporation $44,000,000 every 4 years to lease studios.`  
4. **`Financial Cycle:`**  
   * **`Government:`**  
     * `Constructs structures and subsidizes education.`  
     * `Receives interest-free loans from the bank to subsidize the cost of living for members aged 0-19.`  
     * `Receives rent payments from institutions and lease payments from eyeriS.`  
   * **`eyeriS Corporation:`**  
     * `Uses loans to buy structures, collects rent from institutions, and lease payments from Record Labels.`  
     * `Repays bank loans with interest, which fluctuates based on supply and demand.`  
   * **`Record Labels:`**  
     * `Lease studios from eyeriS, producing content and generating revenue.`  
     * `Payments contribute to eyeriS's revenue, supporting loan repayments and future investments.`  
   * **`Bank:`**  
     * `Provides interest-free loans to the government for subsidizing members aged 0-19.`  
     * `Provides 30-year loans with fluctuating interest rates to eyeriS for purchasing structures.`

### **`Example of a 48-Year Cycle:`**

1. **`Year 0:`**  
   * `Government constructs a studio for $528,000,000.`  
   * `eyeriS takes a $528,000,000 loan from the bank to purchase the studio.`  
2. **`Years 1-4:`**  
   * `Record Labels lease the studio from eyeriS, paying $44,000,000.`  
   * `eyeriS uses the lease payments to repay the loan and cover operational costs.`  
3. **`Years 5-48:`**  
   * `This leasing cycle repeats every 4 years.`  
   * `Institutions continue to pay rent to the government, funded by tuition payments.`  
   * `Government uses interest-free loans from the bank to subsidize living costs for members aged 0-19.`  
4. **`Loan Repayment:`**  
   * `eyeriS repays the bank loan over 30 years, with interest rates adjusted based on market conditions (0.02% to 50%).`  
5. **`End of 48-Year Cycle:`**  
   * `The studio structure despawns.`  
   * `Government constructs a new studio, starting the cycle anew.`

### **`Strategic Implications:`**

* **`Loan Interest Rates:`** `High interest rates increase financial pressure on eyeriS Corporation, especially when structures are scarce. This can lead to higher costs for education and studio leases.`  
* **`Supply and Demand:`** `The availability of structures built by the government directly influences the financial dynamics between eyeriS Corporation, the bank, and Record Labels.`  
* **`Economic Stability:`** `Effective management of loans, interest rates, and resource allocation is crucial for the sustainability of eyeriS Corporation and the overall in-game economy.`  
* **`Government Subsidies:`** `Interest-free loans from the bank ensure the government can continuously support the development and education of young members, maintaining a steady flow of skilled personnel and creators.`
