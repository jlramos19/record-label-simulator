**Record Label Simulator — Game Info**

**THIRD CI — In-game universe lore and game info**

**PLAYER INFO & ROLE**

* The Player is the "Music Executive" of the Record Label.  
* The Player receives requests (in-game main quests) from the CEO of the Record Label.  
* Numerous Rollout Strategies will be used for the Eras of the Acts by the Player, and depending on the Community it's aimed at, its success will vary in efficiency.

**IN-GAME CHARACTERS (IDs)**

* A "CEO" is the founder, owner, and president of the Record Label.  
* An "Act" is the entity that releases the Tracks and Projects (the artist(s)).  
* The "Music Executive" is the Player; the occupation can perform player-specific actions.

**IN-GAME ENTITIES**

**CORPORATIONS**

* A Corporation is the entity that manages and allocates the in-game resources.  
* A Record Label is an in-game building that acts as the Headquarters of in-game operations.  
* These entities are the physical representation of the Company that owns Released Content.

**CREATORS**

Creators are represented in-game by IDs that the Player will have in the Creators tab.

**CREATORS (Roles)**

* A Creator is the role of a musician who creates "Content."  
* A Creator can have up to three Professions: Songwriter, Recorder, and Producer.  
* When equipped to a Studio building: Songwriter creates Sheet Music, Recorder creates Demo Recordings, Producer creates Masters.

**SONGWRITER**

* A Songwriter can have up to 2 preferred lyrical Themes to create Sheet Music.

**RECORDER**

* A Recorder can have up to 2 preferred Moods to create Demo Recordings.

**PRODUCER**

* A Producer can assign a content Genre to a Demo Recording by refining its Theme and Mood, which in turn determines the Quality of the Track.

**ENTITIES’ PROPERTIES**

**ENGAGEMENT REACH & ALIGNMENT**

* Engagement Reach determines how much of the Audience typically engages with the Content put out by the Entity. This property is recalculated and updated in-game dynamically.  
* Alignment is determined by the Entity’s Behavior. Behavior and Alignment can be: "safe", "neutral", "risky".

**AUDIENCE & COMMUNITIES**

**AUDIENCE**

* The Audience is segmented by location.  
* The game has one main Global Audience and three specific Audiences for each country, but they're still part of the Global Audience.  
* This organizes how the in-game Music Charts function.

**COMMUNITY**

* A Community is a portion of the Audience with specific preferences (of Moods and Themes).

**COMMUNITY MEMBERS**

* Community Members are a chunk of 1,000 members with the same properties (population is rounded to the nearest thousand).

**COMMUNITY MEMBERS' PROPERTIES**

**AGE RANGE**

* The Age Range of a Community and its Community Members consists of 4 years per range.  
* A Community can have a broad Age Range, but the Community Members have a specific Age Range of only 4 years.

**TYPICAL SCHEDULE**

* The Typical Schedule determines when the Community Members engage with Released Content.

**PREFERENCES**

* The Preference determines what Released Content the Community Members are likely to engage with.

**IN-GAME AREAS**

* There are three countries: Annglora, Bytenza, and Crowlya.  
* The capital of Annglora is Bloomville. The capital of Bytenza is Belltown. The capital of Crowlya is Campana City.  
* A Studio is a location that can create Content.  
* A Venue is where Performances of Tracks by Acts are performed.

**IN-GAME FACILITIES**

**TYPES OF STUDIOS**

* A Recording Studio is an in-game building with slots that can have Creator IDs equipped to create Content like Sheet Music, Demo Recordings, and Masters.  
* A Broadcast Studio is an in-game building with slots that can have Recorder IDs to create Content like Performance Tape.

**TYPES OF VENUES**

* A Live Venue is a type of venue that requires the Act to show up and perform live. The Player schedules the Recorder ID to do so in the Calendar.  
* A Holographic Venue.

**IN-GAME ITEMS (CONTENT)**

* A Track is an item created by Creators in Studio locations (Main Content).  
* A Project is a collection of Tracks (album).

**CONTENT ORGANIZATION AND INVENTORY**

* Main Folder: Inventory — a digital space for content management, storage, and retrieval by the Music Executive (Player).  
* Inventory Folders: Eras — categories named for a distinct Era.  
* Subfolders in Eras: Projects — groups content like albums.  
* Content in Projects: Files — Tracks, Cover Arts, Music Videos, Interview Tapes, Performance Tapes.  
* File ordering: Tracks (left), then Remixes, then Cover Arts, Music Videos, Performance Tapes, Interview Tapes.

**IN-GAME ITEMS' PROPERTIES**

* Five lyrical Themes: FREEDOM, LOYALTY, AMBITION, MORALITY, POWER.  
* Nine Moods for the instrumental of Tracks: cheering, saddening, thrilling, angering, calming, energizing, uplifting, boring, daring.  
* Forty-five Music Genres: one Genre is the combination of a Theme and a Mood.  
* Example: "Calming Freedom" contains the FREEDOM Theme and the CALMING Mood.  
* Quality is determined by the level of skill required for the Creator to produce content with that property (Theme and/or Mood).

**Preliminary Content**

**PRIMARY Preliminary Content**

* Sheet Music is the first of three steps to create a Track. The Track’s Theme is assigned in this step.

**SECONDARY Preliminary Content**

* Demo Recording is the second of three steps to create a Track. The Track’s Mood is assigned in this step.

**TERTIARY Preliminary Content**

* Master Recordings (Masters) is the third of three steps to create a Track in the Studio by the Producer Creator ID.  
* This step assigns the Track's content Genre and Quality.

**PROMOTIONAL CONTENT**

* Promotional Content is scheduled by the Player.

**GENERAL PASSIVE PROMOTION**

* A Billboard can be scheduled to display in any Area.  
* A Timed Projection can be scheduled to display in any Facility.

**GENERAL ACTIVE PROMOTION**

* An Interview can be scheduled to promote any content in a set Facility.  
* A Live Performance can be scheduled to promote a Track in a Broadcast Studio.

**PROMOTION FOR TRACK ONLY**

* A Music Video is Promotional Content for Tracks.

**IN-GAME QUESTS**

* Quests reward Currency and Expression Points to the Player and the IDs used to complete the Quest.  
* There are Main Quests and Side Quests.

**MAIN QUESTS**

* The CEO gives Main Quests.

**SIDE QUESTS**

* Communities give the Side Quests.

**IN-GAME RESOURCES**

**CURRENCY**

* Currency is what the Player uses to access and influence the Popularity and Alignment of the Record Label’s Entities and Content.

**EXPRESSION POINTS**

* Expression Points are awarded to the Player as they complete in-game Quests and Tasks.

**POPULARITY & ALIGNMENT**

* Popularity determines what things Act(s) have access to. It is measured in Engagement Reach.  
* Alignment also determines what things Act(s) have access to. Alignment is measured by the Act(s) Behavior.

**IN-GAME SYSTEMS**

**Calendar**

* The in-game Calendar allows the Music Executive (Player) to schedule announcements about and releases of Content.  
* The player gameplay starts on "SAT \- JAN 01, 2400 \- 12AM"; the date is formatted and displayed exactly like that, including all-caps.

**Eras**

* An Era is an in-game period during which the Act promotes one or more Projects cohesively within a specific timeframe.

**Types of Eras**

* An Era can be a Flop, Mid, Successful, or Iconic.

**Flop Era**

* Determined dynamically in relation to the previous Era’s performance.

**Mid Era**

* Profitable but Safe Era.  
* Determined by the Act’s previous Era if it remains too similar in terms of its content Genre and the Behavior of the Act(s).

**Successful Era**

* Profitable and Neutral Era.  
* Determined dynamically in relation to the previous Era’s performance.  
* If the Act remains consistent in either content Genre or Behavior but successfully executes a deviance in one of the two that doesn’t stray too far from their established content Genre and Alignment.

**Iconic Era**

* Iconic Era: Profitable and Risky Era.  
* Iconic eras can increase Popularity and earn more Currency and EXP but might decrease Alignment if the Act(s) deviate too much from their established content Genre and Behavior.

**New Acts Exceptions**

* An Era for a New Act without a previous Era is considered Successful or Flop based on Financial Performance.  
* If it loses money (doesn’t exceed Expenses), it is a Flop.  
* If it breaks even or earns money (exceeds Expenses), it is Successful.  
* A New Act cannot have an Iconic Era on its own, but can collaborate with an Established Act to do so.

**Trends**

* A Trend is a popular Music Genre that the Audience is consuming the most of within the current in-game year.  
* Trends are updated weekly, representing last week’s most popular charting Content.

**Types of Trends**

* Cultural Reset: 27 or more weeks.  
* Mid Wave: 9 to 26 weeks.  
* Fad: 1 to 8 weeks.

**eyeriSocial**

* eyeriSocial is the in-game social layer where characters interact digitally; this is one way for the Player to promote Content.

**Programs**

A Program is what Corporations can have to schedule how they broadcast Content. For example, a Broadcast Label can have a Program like a TV Show that takes part in an Area and can have Content broadcasted, which the Audience can consume.

**GAME LOOP**

The Main Game Loop consists of the Player as the Music Executive conducting Acts' Eras successfully by producing high-quality Content and promoting it accordingly.

**DIRECTION STAGE**

* Step 1: Create a New Era.  
* Step 2: Select what content Genre the Era is aiming to represent.  
* Select what Act will execute said content Genre.  
* Select what Alignment the Player wants to represent during that Era.  
* Note: Alignment encapsulates the Project(s), Act(s), and Record Label(s) of that Era.

**CREATION STAGE**

* Step 1: Filter by Act, content Genre, Alignment, and/or Popularity to display IDs relevant to what the Player wants to represent.  
* Step 2: Select relevant IDs to create Content reflective of the content Genre of the Era.  
* Step 3: Place IDs on relevant Areas to create Content.  
* Step 4: Schedule Content for release.

**PROMOTION STAGE**

* Step 1: Assign Budget to Promotional Efforts.  
* Step 2: Promote Content being released by advertising (for example: eyeriSocial posts by relevant Entities).  
* Promote Release Date of Content by announcing it.  
* Promote content Genre of Content to influence Audience perception of the Era.  
* Promote Act(s) associated with Content to influence the Popularity of the Era.  
* Promote Behavior associated with Act(s) to gain Alignment.

**LEGACY STAGE**

* Step 1: Schedule the in-game date for the Era's conclusion.  
* Step 2: View the final Performance & Finance report.  
* Performance shows if Popularity increased, decreased, or stayed the same.  
* Finance shows if the Budget reflected a Loss, broke even, or Earned money.  
* Adhering to the target preference presets of the Era grants extra EXP (Expression Points) but might hinder Currency earnings if Trends change or the Content is received negatively or ignored.
